Project Management

UM Office of Research & Sponsored Projects

Understanding the Problem

Through a series of contextual interviews with employees from all levels and creating an affinity wall we were able to idenfity the source of the ORSP's communication issues.

Finding Solutions

After identifying these pain points we developed solutions to reduce information overload in ORSP employee email inboxes and improve client inquiry response times. Our recommendations included:

  • Maintaining separate inboxes for in-office communication and client communication (or use other software such as Slack for in office communication)
  • Utilizing labels and folders to identify clients more efficiently
  • Develop office-wide protocol for email organization to prevent the loss of information and maintain consistency

UX Design

Detroit Design Summit: Decrypt Detroit

Needs Assessment

Through research and interviews my team identified three primary needs of Detroit students:

  • Engaging and interactive learning experiences
  • Connectivity to the cultural center
  • An avenue to contribute their own ideas and creativity within the cultural center

Vision and solution

Our research on the cultural district, technological access, and physical transportation in Detroit and interviews with two high school teachers led to our focus on a digital experience that builds on an understanding of how students use technology to form social connections and have fun utilizing interactive technologies to engage with their community. We drew inspiration from other digital games that allow users to collaborate and be creative such as MineCraft and Fortnite.

Decrypt Detroit is a digitally-based competition spanning the cultural district. The application provides "missions" for students to complete throughout the district, challenging users to visit all of the institutions to complete scavenger hunts and create challenges unique to each location. Scavenger hunts unique to each institution will lead players through each location, and points can be earned by finding and scanning QR codes throughout the institutions.

To begin the game, players join a team with their friends or other online users and compete against different teams to complete the most missions and claim ‘zones’ marked by digital displays placed in front of each institution throughout the district. Along the way, players are invited to express their unique perspective on Detroit by sharing photos that respond to historical and cultural prompts. These prompts invite users to share their own perspective on the city of Detroit and allow their voices to be heard and seen throughout the district. Responses to cultural prompts will be made visible on the digital displays placed outside of each institution. The game culminates in a winning team based on the points earned throughout scavenger hunts and creative challenges in each institution or ‘zone’.

Web Application Development

Garden Works

Website Vision and Design

Garden Works is a small organic farm located in Ann Arbor, MI. The goal of this project is to develop a website for the farm with product availability and online order components using Square. The website will be developed using Django.

Application Development

The foundation for the site will began with building databases to store product information, customer information, and order information.

Following the creation of those databases, I will develop an order summary of the items contained in a shopping cart.

In the third step I will create a checkout form, allowing users to submit an order and allowing the order accessed and saved in the database.

Lastly, I will implement payment processing using Square.
